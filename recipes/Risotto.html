Risotto

Home

This is a classic, creamy risotto from Northern Italy. The key to a perfect risotto is patience—slowly adding warm broth and stirring constantly helps the rice release its starches, creating a luxuriously creamy texture without any actual cream. It's a simple yet elegant dish that is incredibly comforting.

Ingredients

  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • ½ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup Arborio rice

Steps

  1. In a medium pot, heat the broth and keep it at a gentle simmer.
  2. In a medium pot, heat the broth and keep it at a gentle simmer.
  3. Add the Arborio rice and stir for 1-2 minutes until the grains are lightly toasted and coated in the oil.
  4. If using, pour in the white wine and stir until it is fully absorbed by the rice.
  5. Add one ladle of warm broth to the rice. Stir constantly until the liquid is absorbed.
  6. Continue adding broth one ladle at a time, stirring continuously and allowing each addition to be absorbed before adding the next.
  7. Cook for about 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is creamy but still slightly al dente.
  8. Remove from heat. Stir in the remaining 1 tbsp of butter and the grated Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve immediately.
