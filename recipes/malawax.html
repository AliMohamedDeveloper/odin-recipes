Malawax (also spelled Malawah) is a sweet, tender, and slightly elastic pancake that is a staple of Somali cuisine. Unlike American pancakes, it is typically made as one large, thin pancake that is cooked in a single layer in a pan. It has a uniquely soft, chewy texture and is often flavored with cardamom and sometimes sugar. It is commonly eaten for breakfast or as a snack, served with tea, honey, butter, or even a dollop of sour cream. Its delightful flavor and simple ingredients have made it popular across the Horn of Africa and beyond.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (adjust to taste)
  • Â¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup milk (or water for a lighter version)
  • Butter or oil, for greasing the pan

Steps

  1. Whisk dry ingredients (flour, sugar, salt, cardamom) in a bowl.
  2. Add the egg and half the milk. Whisk into a smooth batter.
  3. Whisk in the remaining milk until the batter is thin.
  4. Let the batter rest for 15 minutes.
  5. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat with a little butter.
  6. Pour in a ladle of batter and quickly swirl the pan to coat the bottom.
  7. Cook for 1-2 minutes until set and the bottom is golden. Do not flip.
  8. Slide onto a plate and serve warm with honey and butter.
