Sonalli Spaghetti Suqaar

Home

Somali Spaghetti is a vibrant and flavorful fusion dish that beautifully blends Italian pasta with traditional Somali spices. The key is the "suugo," a rich, tomato-based sauce often simmered with minced meat (suqaar), garlic, ginger, cumin, and coriander. It's a beloved comfort food for many Somali families, typically served for lunch or dinner and often enjoyed on weekends.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti or linguine
  • 1 lb beef or lamb, cut into small cubes (suqaar) or use ground meat
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 (15 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • Vegetable oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, for garnish

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package directions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil over medium-high heat. Add the meat and cook until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add more oil if needed. Sauté the onion until soft. Add garlic, ginger, and bell pepper; cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and spices (cumin, coriander, turmeric, cloves), cooking for another minute.
  5. Return the meat to the pot. Add the tomato sauce and 1 cup of water. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes, until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  7. Pour the sauce over the cooked spaghetti and toss to combine. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.
